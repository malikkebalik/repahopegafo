Khabib Nurmagomedov releases harrowing footage of brutal weight cut before Justin Gaethje win at UFC

Khabib Nurmagomedov has released footage of the brutal weight cut he went through in order to hit the 155lbs limit for his fight against Justin Gaethje.

The Russian submitted Gaethje in the second round of their enthralling match-up at UFC 254 on Fight Island, before announcing his retirement from the sport for good.

Despite standing at 5ft 10ins, Khabib has historically struggled to make the weight limit for lightweight and reportedly has as much as 40lbs to cut before fights.

However, he has always remained a consummate professional and put in the graft to ensure he always hits the scales bang on weight.

However, the footage released by ‘The Eagle’ showing him literally sweating out the last few kilograms before facing Gaethje makes for tough viewing as the 32-year-old rapidly loses strength.

Wearing a sweat suit whilst riding a bike is a sure fire way to lose a final few pounds, but Khabib and his team have to go even further and douse the champion in towels while he remains wrapped in layers.

[youtube https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3hlRC4AHs4k]

As the weight gradually begins to fall off, Khabib practically wilts in the hotel room under the duress and has to be helped to his feet by his training partners and it makes for uncomfortable viewing.

In 2018, Khabib admitted he suffered seizures while he was cutting weight before UFC 229 when he went and defeated Conor McGregor, but moving up to the more comfortable weight of 170lbs to fight at welterweight never appealed to the 29-0 superstar.

Prior to facing Gaethje, Khabib revealed he would finish his career as a lightweight and that he had no plans to move up in weight.

“I am a natural lightweight,” he said. “My career in the UFC started as lightweight and will end here.

“I don’t want to jump to other divisions, I don’t want to experiment. I have been doing very well in the lightweight division for eight years.

“And I want to finish my career in this weight category.”
