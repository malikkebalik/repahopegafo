Matthew Stafford Bio: NFL &amp; Injury [2023 Update]

Matthew Stafford, originally John Matthew Stafford, is a quarterback for the Los Angeles Rams of the NFL and has played for the team since 2021.

He formerly played for the Detroit Lions from 2009 to 2020 and parted ways with the team on mutual agreement.

After being traded to the Chargers, Stafford led the team to victory in Super Bowl LVI.

He ranks in the top 20 for all-time in passing yards, passing touchdowns, pass attempts, and completions.

Similarly, he holds the record for the fastest player to reach 40,000 passing yards and ranks fourth in all-time passing yards per game.

Stafford is one of the most highlighted players in the current NFL scene but has been out since he sustained a neck injury in the Rams’ Week 11 loss to the New Orleans Saints in November 2022. He was evaluated for a concussion and left the game.

It was initially guaranteed that he would miss at least four games, but according to the head coach Sean McVay, Stafford is likely out for the remainder of the year.

Matthew Stafford Bio | Early Life & Education

The Rams quarterback Matthew Stafford was born John Matthew Stafford on February 7, 1988, in Tampa, Florida, United States, to his father, John Stafford, and mother, Margaret Stafford.

He grew up with his only sibling, sister Page Stafford.

His family lived in Dunwoody, Georgia, initially, where his father pursued his higher education at the University of Georgia.

His family afterward moved to Dallas, Texas.

Matthew went to Highland Park High School in Dallas County, Texas, with Los Angeles Dodgers pitcher Clayton Kershaw.

He played high school football for Highland Park Scots under the head coach Randy Allen.

Matt was considered to be one of the best high school QBs in the States in the Class of 2006.

As a matter of fact, Rivals.com listed Matt as the No. 1 pro-style quarterback prospect of his class and a five-star recruit.

After an exceptional high school football, Matthew went to the University of Georgia in January 2006.

He wore jersey number 7 and became the first true freshman QB to start for the Georgia Bulldogs since Quincy Carter in 1998.

He performed super impressive in his freshman year and finished the season with 1,749 passing yards, 13 interceptions, and seven touchdowns.

Injury

The Los Angeles Rams have placed Matthew Stafford on injured reserve, and he will likely miss the whole season.

According to sources, he would be eligible to return to the active roster in Week 17.

He has not played since he sustained a neck injury in a game against New Orleans Saints in Week 11.

After being evaluated for a concussion, he left the game early.

According to the Rams head coach, Sean McVay, after taking the hit in the game, he felt some numbness in his legs.

While some analysts and pundits have said that his concussion might affect Matt’s 2023 season, head coach Sean has noted that there currently is no concern that Matt’s neck issue would linger into something that would affect the forthcoming season.

Matthew Stafford Bio | Career

Matt had an exceptional college football which made him forgo his senior season at college and enter the 2009 NFL Draft.

Football analyst Mel Kiper Jr. had predicted that Matt would be the first pick in the NFL Draft way back in time, which came out true as Matthew was selected in the first round by the Detroit Lions in the 2009 NFL Draft.

Matt became the first rookie quarterback since Greg Landry in 1968 to start in Week 1.

The following season he injured his right shoulder in the 19-14 loss against the Bears.

Nevertheless, he returned on October 31 against Redskins and threw for 212 yards, one interception, and four touchdowns.

And after twelve years with the Lions, Matt was traded to the Rams in a package involving Jared Goff, the 2022 Walter Payton NFL Man of the Year nominee for the Detroit Lions.

In January 2021, Matt signed a contract with the Rams.

He made his Rams debut during Sunday Night Football against the Bears.
