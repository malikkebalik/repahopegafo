Biography

Mario Cuomo was born in New York City, NY on June 15, 1932. American politician and Democrat who, between 1975 and 1994, served as New York’s Secretary of State, Lieutenant Governor, and Governor. He came to national prominence after giving the keynote speech at the 1984 Democratic National Convention in which he criticized the policies of then-President Ronald Reagan. He and David Paterson were both Governors of New York. He graduated first in his class from St. John’s University.
