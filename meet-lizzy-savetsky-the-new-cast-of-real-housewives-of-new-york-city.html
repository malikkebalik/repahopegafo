Lizzy Savetsky whose husband is a renowned plastic surgeon has signed the Bravo’s reality TV series Real Housewives of New York.

Who is Lizzy Savetsky?

Lizzy Savetsky is a Texas native also known as Elizabeth. She is a fashion influencer and social media star based in New York City. She is a Miss Teen Texas participant who attended NYU’s Gallatin School of Individualized Study.

Lizzy had an amazing past where she travel around the world, Israel was something she tried against her parents’ will. Meanwhile, her journey as a cheerleader and country music singer in her college days was something she often recalls.

She is beginning her new reality TV journey as a mother of three kids, two daughters, and a son. Residing in New York she often posts about accessories and Jewish culture.

Being vocal in the Yiddish language to 200k followers on Instagram she rose to stardom. Her IGTV videos received thousands of views and likes but her anxiety and depression after her kids and success did not change at all as she said,

“Now I have everything I’ve ever worked for. Another child, a successful career. I should be happy. But she wasn’t. She was struggling with anxiety and depression.”

Bravo TV is replacing the original casts of Real Housewives of New York

Who are the casts that Bravo TV is replacing? Real Housewives fans must be quite aware that RHONY is replacing a few casts as sources previously disclosed. Let’s hear what the sources revealed earlier,

“Bravo found people aren’t responding as well to the constant bickering and fighting of the [previous] cast,’ and are ‘hoping that this new cast will be a bit more inspiring and attract the younger audience.”

Bravo has fired all the casts with the intent to bring them in the new legacy so but not the RHONY they used to feature. Well, the insider added that the casts who have established their own brands are no longer to repeat in the show saying,

“The newcomers to the cast of the series  are set to replace Luann de Lesseps, Leah McSweeney, Sonja Morgan, Ramona Singer, and Eboni K. Williams – women who have their own brands, or at least strong Instagram followings’ and are ‘already strong in the influencer space.”
