Truck driver, Frank Ingram Towing killed in crash on I-575 south in Cherokee County

In a devastating incident on Interstate 575 South in Cherokee County, Georgia, the towing community mourns the loss of Frank Ingram, a dedicated tow truck driver from Ingram Wrecker Service. Frank Ingram tragically lost his life today in a deadly accident while responding to a wrecker call.

The accident occurred around 1:10 p.m., leading to the closure of lanes on I-575 southbound between Sixes Road (exit 11) and Ridgeway Parkway (exit 9). Frank Ingram was struck and killed by a passing vehicle while diligently performing his duties, leaving the entire community in shock and sorrow.

Ledbetter Wrecker, deeply affected by the tragedy, expressed their condolences, stating, “The Ledbetter Wrecker team would like to offer our deepest condolences to the Ingram Wrecker Service family and friends. Today Frank Ingram was struck and killed by a passing vehicle on 575 while working. This is heartbreaking news to have to post about.”

Emergency personnel worked diligently at the crash scene, causing significant delays as they tended to the aftermath of the accident. The collision resulted in the fatality of a tow truck operator, underscoring the dangers faced by those in the towing profession.

While lanes were temporarily closed, every effort was made to clear the area swiftly. As the investigation continues, the exact timeline for reopening the lanes remains uncertain. The entire community, especially those within the towing industry, mourns the loss of Frank Ingram and extends heartfelt sympathy to his family, friends, and colleagues during this incredibly difficult time.
